Governed AI

AI that assists every reviewer — and decides nothing.

Enstellar uses AI to remove the busywork around clinical review, never to perform it. No inference output is ever the sole basis for a denial, a partial denial, or any adverse action. Every adverse determination requires recorded human sign-off — and clinician sign-off wherever your policies or regulators require it.

What AI does

  • Produces a cited summary of the case, so reviewers start with the evidence already organized.
  • Extracts structured entities and flags documentation gaps before a case stalls.
  • Surfaces conflicts in the record and suggests triage — with a confidence level attached.

What AI never does

  • Issues a determination, or commits any state transition on its own.
  • Asserts a clinical or administrative claim without a citation to its source.
  • Operates outside the endpoints authorized for your tenant and compliance boundary.
Auditable
by design

Every AI interaction records the model, the prompt and template versions, the inputs it referenced, the output it produced, its confidence, and the action your reviewer took — accept, edit, reject, or override, with a reason. If a determination is ever questioned, you can show exactly what the AI offered and exactly what the human decided.

Turn AI off by tenant, workflow, role, or boundary — and every workflow keeps running. AI is never required to operate.

The reviewer's workspace

Every reviewer works the whole case in one place.

No tab-switching for member context, no chasing documents across systems, no determination that slips out without the rationale behind it. Enstellar gives nurses and medical directors a single workspace where the evidence, the rules, and the decision live together.

01

Nurse review

A reviewer opens a worklist sorted by SLA risk and lands on a case with the member, coverage, service lines, documents, the rules trace from policy, and the AI advisory summary already assembled. They approve within scope, issue a structured RFI, or escalate — with rationale captured every time.

02

Adverse determination

When criteria aren't met, the case routes to a medical director where required. The engine will not let a denial, partial denial, or modification be issued until the required sign-off, rationale, and citations are recorded and the notification artifacts are ready.

The guardrail is enforced — not a reminder
03

Peer-to-peer

Schedule it, document it, and keep it attached to the case timeline — so the conversation that changed the outcome is part of the record, not a note in someone's inbox.

Reviewers spend their time on clinical judgment. The system handles the clocks, the routing, the documentation trail, and the proof.

Provenance

Every decision is defensible the day you make it — and the day it's appealed.

Criteria change. Policies version. Reviewers move on. Enstellar preserves the exact rule version, evidence, and rationale available at the moment of each decision — so a determination is judged by what was known then, not by what changed since.

01 / AT DECISION TIME

Decisions are audited against the criteria that existed at decision time. No retroactive ambiguity about which policy applied.

02 / FAIR APPEALS

Appeals reviewers see the original rule trace separately from any later updates. The appeal is fair because the record is honest.

03 / FULL TRACE

Every rule result, document, AI extraction, communication, and action carries its source provenance. A case reconstructs end to end from its own event history.

Because the workflow state machine is the system of record, nothing happens off the books — every transition, clock event, and sign-off is an immutable, time-stamped event.
